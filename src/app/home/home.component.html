FAQ

Q: What is this lawsuit about and why is there a settlement?

A: The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (“EEOC”) is a federal agency that enforces several federal laws prohibiting discrimination in employment on the basis of several protected categories, including race, color, national origin, sex, religion, disability, age, and genetic information (read more about EEOC at www.eeoc.gov). EEOC brought this lawsuit under the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (“ADEA”). EEOC’s lawsuit, filed in the United States District Court for the Southern District of Florida, Case No. 15-cv-20561-JAL/JG, alleged that Seasons 52 discriminated on the basis of age in hiring at 35 of its newly opened restaurants.

On May 3, 2018, the Court approved a Consent Decree resolving the lawsuit that includes $2.85 million in relief for aggrieved individuals and significant injunctive relief, including changes to Seasons 52’s policies and training.

Q: How do I make a claim?

A: In order to make a claim and participate in the settlement, you MUST either fill out and post- mark the questionnaire you received in the mail OR fill out the questionnaire on this website by [DEADLINE]. All claims must be submitted either by mail or online by this date. On-line forms received after this date, and paper forms post-marked after this date, will likely not be considered. Anyone whose claim is submitted after this date will likely not be entitled to any monetary relief. Therefore, you should submit your claim form as soon as possible. If returning via mail, you should consider using expedited delivery service and mail tracking.

Q: How will I know that I am eligible/how much money will I receive?

A: After the deadline for return of the claim forms, EEOC will determine eligibility and will notify claimants of the amount of their monetary award (if any). Analysis of the claims forms will take some time. You should not expect to receive notification of the determination of a monetary award for a number of months after the deadline date. If EEOC determines that you are eligible for a monetary award, the notification letter will include a release of claims that you must sign and return to obtain any monetary relief. Any monetary relief probably will not be paid until [DATE].

Q: What happens if I am interested in working for Seasons 52?

A: If you are interested in reapplying for employment with Seasons 52 for a front of house or back of house position, you can apply online on Seasons 52’s website for any vacancy at any Seasons 52 location. You will be considered along with any other applicant and will NOT be subjected to either age discrimination or retaliation for having participated in this lawsuit as a Claimant. However, an individual’s eligibility for an award does not mean that the same individual is presently qualified for employment with Seasons 52 in a front of house and/or back of house position. Eligibility does not guarantee or secure a position with Seasons 52 for any individual now, or in the future.
